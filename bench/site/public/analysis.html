3. The four failure classes

ABSORB Closure-absorption deficit — 22 sequences, the dominant class

We detect the same revisits OKVIS2 does (often more reliably), verify them geometrically, and then extract a fraction of the value. Mechanism: our closures act on the estimate through (a) a rigid CORR step or pose-graph deform of stored keyframes, (b) a transient pose prior (TIGHT), and (c) fixed-3D landmark reprojection factors (LMFACT/LMTRACK) whose scene-gated fold into the marginalization prior is permanent and irreversible. OKVIS2 instead un-marginalizes: its pose-graph edges cache the common observations that created them, and a closure turns edges back into live landmarks + observations, then runs an asynchronous optimization of the whole loop. Every closure there triggers joint re-estimation of structure and poses with fresh linearization; every closure here applies a rigid transform plus factors that pull toward PnP-derived 3D — the same 3D whose error is the residual we are trying to remove (the LOOPBURST post-mortem showed this circularity precisely: multi-frame fusion of the same biased single-frame solves reaches 90%+ consensus on the bias).

TRACK Local-tracking degradation — 9 sequences

RTE (6-frame local precision) is our strength — better than OKVIS2 on 24 of 39 sequences, often 2×. The exceptions are exactly the hard-motion sequences: MOO01/MOO02 (1.9×), MOO13 (2.5×), V2_03 (1.3×), corridors (1.3–1.6×), room1/room5 (1.4–1.6×). Basalt's KLT patch-flow frontend degrades under motion blur, aggressive rotation, and low-texture walls, where OKVIS2's BRISK detect-and-redescribe frontend re-acquires. KLT loses the patch and the track dies; a descriptor frontend re-detects the same corner a frame later. The two headset walk-arounds (MOO01/02) and the fast difficult rooms (V2_03, room5) are precisely where flow tracks shear.

DETECT Closure-detection misses — 5 sequences

On MH_02, MH_05, V1_03, V2_02, V2_03 our map layer applied zero closures (loops=0 in run telemetry) while OKVIS2+LC gained up to 2.1× from its own. The V-room sequences are small rooms with continuous re-observation — precisely where our sub-gate machinery (TIGHTSUB) should be feeding factors continuously, but our retrieval-triggered pipeline needs a VPR hit + NN prematch + LighterGlue verify to fire at all, and fast blurred queries fail retrieval. OKVIS2 does not need "an event": every keyframe matches against co-visible map landmarks by descriptor as part of normal tracking. Detection-by-retrieval vs detection-by-covisibility is a structural difference, not a tuning difference.

ROBUST Their fragility, our discipline — 8 sequences

OKVIS2+LC's absorption power has no safety: on slides1 its own loop closure makes it 3.7× worse (8.6→31.8), V2_01 1.4× worse, slides2/MOO09 slightly worse, and corridor5/magistrale1/drive2 blow up outright. Our gate discipline (2-frame confirm, verification caps, scene-gated folds, alias margins) has never produced a catastrophic closure in ~2,000 logged runs — our map gain is never <0.91×. This is the moat: it is why we win precisely where they explode, and it must be preserved by any absorption upgrade.

5. Mechanism review — the full stack, ours vs theirs

Frontend: Basalt KLT patch flow vs OKVIS2 BRISK detect-and-describe AHEAD on smooth motion · BEHIND under blur

Basalt tracks sparse patches with pyramidal inverse-compositional KLT between frames; features live as long as flow converges. This gives sub-pixel local precision — our RTE beats OKVIS2 on 24/39 sequences, down to 0.39× — but a shed track is gone: under motion blur or texture loss the patch pool collapses and drift spikes (MOO01/02 RTE 1.9×, MOO13 2.5×, V2_03, room1/5, corridors 1.3–1.6×). OKVIS2 detects BRISK corners every keyframe and matches by descriptor: worse local noise (their RTE), but re-acquisition is free — a corner lost to blur is re-found the next sharp frame, and matching against map landmarks doubles as continuous closure detection. The XFeat/LighterGlue/EigenPlaces stack we built lives only in the map layer, not in the VIO frontend.

VIO backend: Basalt square-root sliding-window vs OKVIS2 bounded factor graph PARITY in mechanism, BEHIND in closure interface

Basalt: fixed-lag window (frames + keyframes), landmarks eliminated by nullspace projection, marginalization prior kept as a matrix square root updated by specialized QR (Demmel 2021) — numerically excellent, fast, and closed: once states leave the window their information is folded irreversibly into one dense prior. OKVIS2: bounded factor graph of observations + IMU preintegrals + pose-graph edges created by marginalizing common observations, cached so they can be turned back into landmarks and observations (Leutenegger 2022). The difference is not estimation quality — it is that their marginalization is a reversible container and ours is a one-way sink. Every downstream absorption difference follows from this single design divergence.

Closure detection: retrieval-event pipeline vs covisibility matching AHEAD at long range · BEHIND at short range

Ours: EigenPlaces VPR shortlist → NN prematch → LighterGlue → PnP verify → 2-frame confirm. Superb at kidnapped/wake-up and long-range revisits (95% burst recall, corridor5/slides where their recognition fails). But it is an event pipeline: no VPR hit, no closure — and fast blurred queries produce no VPR hit (EuRoC V-rooms: loops=0 on four sequences). OKVIS2 matches live BRISK features against co-visible map landmarks continuously as part of tracking — closure detection at room scale is free and permanent. We built TIGHTSUB to emulate continuous absorption but it still requires the retrieval event to fire first.

Closure application: rigid correction + priors + irreversible folds vs landmark reactivation + async loop optimization THE dominant gap

Ours, in order of maturity: (1) CORR rigid step / pose-graph deform of stored keyframes — moves the map, injects zero information into the estimator; (2) TIGHT pose prior — transient, absorbed then forgotten; (3) LMFACT/LMTRACK fixed-3D reprojection factors — per-point, optimizer-arbitrated, but pulling toward PnP-derived 3D (the closure's own estimate, bias included), transient unless folded; (4) LMMARG scene-gated fold — permanent injection into the sqrt prior, one-way, no relinearization, gated hard because a wrong fold is forever (four closure-quality gate families failed before the scene gate held). Net measured conversion: 1.0–1.2× on most sequences, 2–3.8× at best. Theirs: the closure reopens the past — pose-graph edges revert to observations, old landmarks become live variables again, and the whole loop is re-optimized asynchronously with consistent relinearization, then synchronized into the realtime graph. Net measured conversion: 1.7–31.9×. They also pay for it (slides1 0.27×, V2_01 0.73× — reopened wrong loops poison everything); our discipline never pays. The target architecture is their absorption behind our gates.

Relocalization / wake-up DECISIVELY AHEAD

Burst-15 recall 95.1% mean over 17 sequences, ~55ms, 3–5cm; OKVIS2's reference harness deadlocks at the sensor gap (DNF on all six blackout arms). Not contested by any measurement we have.

Map maintenance: LOCALBA, welds, eviction, descriptors ADEQUATE, not the bottleneck

Bounded structure refinement (LOCALBA resection–intersection), submap welds with age rules, cap-400 slots with usefulness eviction, XFeat descriptors with union banks. The reloc grid (rooms 100% recall @ 3–5cm) says map quality is sufficient for recognition; the absorption analysis says the map's metric content — per-keyframe landmark copies refined only at closure events — is below BA-grade, which is what caps LMFACT factor quality (factors are only as good as the 3D they pull toward). A landmark-reactivation architecture fixes this automatically because structure re-enters optimization.

What we have not touched, in rough order of relevance to the measured gaps:

  • The marginalization schedule itself — which frames/keyframes marginalize when, and irreversibly. DM-VIO (Stumberg 2022) demonstrates a second, delayed marginalization graph that can be re-advanced with new information and consistent linearization points — the direct antidote to our "posts lose the state-window race" and "folds are forever" problems.
  • Landmark lifetime — Basalt landmarks die when their host keyframe marginalizes; there is no reactivation path. OKVIS2's whole absorption advantage lives in this one difference.
  • Relinearization — the sqrt prior keeps first-estimate Jacobians; large closure corrections make the prior inconsistent with the corrected state (we measured the symptom as "fold poison" in aliased spaces and gated it, where OKVIS2 relinearizes it away).
  • The optical-flow frontend — patch sizes, pyramid levels, and track-loss recovery (the MSD frontend sweep tested config knobs, not architecture: a redetect-and-redescribe fallback under blur was never tried).
  • Keyframe selection under fast motion — Basalt keyframes by flow disparity; blur-heavy sections under-keyframe exactly when redundancy is most needed.

7. Literature

  • OKVIS2 — Leutenegger, Realtime Scalable Visual-Inertial SLAM with Loop Closure (arXiv:2202.09199): pose-graph edges by marginalizing common observations, fluidly reverted to landmarks+observations at closure; async loop optimization synchronized into the realtime graph. The measured 1.7–31.9× conversion machine.
  • DM-VIO — von Stumberg & Cremers, Delayed Marginalization Visual-Inertial Odometry (arXiv:2201.04114): a second factor graph with delayed marginalization, re-advanced to inject information into already-marginalized states with consistent linearization. The template for making Basalt's prior reversible without abandoning its sqrt form.
  • Square-root marginalization — Demmel et al., Square Root Marginalization for Sliding-Window Bundle Adjustment (ICCV 2021, arXiv:2109.02182): the exact machinery our stages 4–7 graft onto (nullspace projection, QR prior updates); algebraically equivalent to Schur complement, 36% faster.
  • Basalt / NFR — Usenko et al., Visual-Inertial Mapping with Non-Linear Factor Recovery: Basalt's own answer to map reuse recovers nonlinear factors from the VIO for an offline mapping layer — evidence the authors also considered the VIO window too closed; NFR is the non-causal cousin of what we need causally.
  • Learned-dense frontier — BAMF-SLAM (RAFT-style recurrent matching, multi-fisheye) and P2U-SLAM report sub-10cm magistrale numbers on TUM-VI — with offline/full-BA, non-causal protocols and start-segment-only alignment; not comparable to causal numbers here, but they mark where dense learned matching is heading. Our XFeat-dense NPU frontend is the on-device cousin of this direction.

9. Limitations of this analysis

  • OKVIS2 numbers are our own runs of their reference implementation with per-dataset configs; a tuned proprietary deployment may differ. All comparisons are same-machine, same-protocol.
  • okvis2-lc0 as "their VIO" includes their keyframe BA; it is the correct absorption baseline but not a pure odometry lower bound.
  • Corridor/magistrale medians carry residual bimodality even at n=10–20; per-seq deltas under ~1.3× in those groups should not be over-read (the corr10/mag10 power rounds bound this).
  • MSD n=3 per cell for EuRoC/MSD arms; tight ranges (reported per-card) justify the reads, but MOO03's [15.6–24.7] spread means its exact ratio is soft.
  • The RTE→frontend attribution rests on RTE being closure-independent (6-frame deltas); closure snaps can leak into RTE at application instants — the reported medians are robust to a handful of snap frames.
