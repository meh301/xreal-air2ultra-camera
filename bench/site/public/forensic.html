Why the tracker underperforms — a line-level review

00Did DBoW2 beat us?

No — and the real answer is more useful than "yes".

The dbw19 arm swapped only the retrieval front-end; our verify + matching + PnP ran unchanged in both arms. 86.5% vs 88.8% over 30-probe runs is inside binomial noise. What it actually exposed: our NPU-fed learned VPR buys nothing over 2012 bag-of-words, because both are starved by the same bottleneck downstream of retrieval. Improving the retrieval engine cannot help until the geometric gates below it are opened.

Two measurement artifacts were inflating the panic, both now fixed: the keyframe-cap override was silently ignored until 2026-07-17 (so every prior magistrale number was measured at a hard 400-cap, not the intended cap), and a pooled "long" median was cancelling a real 2x corridor loss (18.0 vs 9.2 cm) against magistrale. The ORB-SLAM3 baseline is a dead system (MH_01 870 cm, LC a byte-identical no-op) and is now quarantined. But the gap to OKVIS2+LC is genuine — their corridor 2.8 cm / room 1.2 cm match their published numbers. No comfort there.

01Executive diagnosis

The system underperforms not for lack of the right algorithms but because the right algorithms are built and then disabled, and because the map->VIO coupling is architected as a cascade of hard gates that strip or reject the very information a loop closure carries. Four threads run through every confirmed finding. (1) Reference-grade robustness machinery ships OFF by default: landmark recall, 4-DOF gravity-locked pose-graph (PGO4DOF), DCS/switchable robust closure kernels, coverage-aware eviction (COVKEEP), long-term-keyframe anchoring, FARBEAR far-structure reloc, TRUSTVPR/LighterGlue, and even the -DXR_MAP_MAX_KF cap override (verifiably inert until 2026-07-17) are all implemented but flag-gated, so every scored run used a crippled default configuration. (2) The pipeline is a chain of all-or-nothing hard thresholds where the field uses continuous uncertainty weighting -- a 6 m depth cut, a covis>=3 floor, an additive match margin, a binary closure gate, absolute cosine thresholds, a 400-KF hard cap -- each dropping true correspondences before geometry can arbitrate, so failures compound multiplicatively and are engine-independent, which is exactly why swapping DBoW2->MegaLoc yields no gain: the bottleneck sits downstream of retrieval. (3) The closure channel is structurally self-defeating: map factors drive the solved increment yet are excluded from the LM accept/reject test (so the optimizer rejects precisely the meter-scale corrections closures induce), are weighted 16x below a raw KLT track with a 6x looser Huber knee, are linearized at a moving target with J=I, and have no retained in-window keyframe to attach to -- over-determining the measured 1.0-1.2x absorption versus OKVIS2's 1.7-31.9x. (4) Capacity dominates the magistrale collapse: 400 slots at 0.30 m spacing is ~120 m of coverage against 460-920 m sequences, so the closure target is evicted before the revisit -- a capacity artifact, not an algorithmic limit, and one the measurement stack has been masking (a pooled 'long' median hides the 2x corridor loss of 18.0 vs 9.2 cm, a broken orb3 baseline pollutes fleet-complete claims, and two scorers disagree on divergence). Fix the configuration and the gates before touching the retrieval engine.

04Attack order

  1. 1
    FIRST fix the measurement harness -- no downstream A/B is trustworthy until it is: (a) verify -DXR_MAP_MAX_KF/EXTRA actually propagates into the replay binary (inert until 2026-07-17, so prior magistrale numbers may be 400-cap artifacts, not algorithm results); (b) split the pooled 'long' site group into corridor vs magistrale/slides so the 2x corridor loss (18.0 vs 9.2 cm) stops being cancelled by a pooled median; (c) delete the RTE divergence term at score.py:119 so fastbench and the site share one ATE-only scorer; (d) quarantine the broken orb3 baseline (MH_01 870 cm, LC a byte-identical no-op on TUM-VI) so fleet-complete claims are not made against a dead system.
  2. 2
    Raise the keyframe cap to sequence size (2000-4000) for offline replay and enable+harden coverage-aware eviction (XR_COVKEEP, never evict a cell's sole occupant while a redundant KF exists); re-score magistrale/corridor reloc at each cap. This is the single largest lever on the magistrale collapse AND the precondition for any closure-absorption gain, because you cannot close against an evicted keyframe.
  3. 3
    Fix the LM cost/ratio exclusion (rank 2): add the XR pose-prior + fixed-3D factor error to both error_total and after_error_total and the factor model-cost-change to l_diff. Highest-leverage estimator change -- it stops the optimizer from rejecting exactly the corrections closures induce and should shift corridor behaviour from the bimodal bad mode toward acceptance.
  4. 4
    Open the reloc correspondence stack as one A/B batch (all engine-independent and compounding): soften the 6 m depth cap to a depth-uncertainty weight or default-enable FARBEAR/P3P (rank 3); lower COVIS_MIN_KF to 1 + strong-inlier-ratio (rank 4); replace the additive match margin with a Lowe ratio moved AFTER RANSAC (rank 5); add the guided-reprojection second matching pass (rank 7); bump RELOC_TOPK to 6-8 (resize the fixed cand arrays). Measure magistrale/corridor reloc recall -- expect the biggest recall jump here.
  5. 5
    Complete the absorption fix on the factor side: set map-factor sigma to real feature noise (~1 px, w~1) via XR_LMF_SIGMA (rank 6) and correct the tight-prior Jacobian (capture absolute P once, r=Log(T^-1 P), full 6x6 blocks with the SE(3) right-Jacobian) (rank 9). Re-run the corridor absorption A/B targeting OKVIS2-class 1.7x+ absorption.
  6. 6
    Enable the dormant robustness features as controlled A/Bs now that the harness and gates are fixed: landmark recall (fallback-only, watch the never-deleted-patches TODO for memory growth), 4-DOF gravity locking for all deform/relax writebacks (fix the misleading pgo_blend full-quaternion path), DCS/switchable robust closure kernel (eg_relax, minding the self-drift-correlated-edge caveat), and long-term-keyframe anchoring on confirmed revisits (rank 10).
  7. 7
    Attack the frontend tracking-noise floor the ledger blames for the residual corridor gap: replace the gated ZNCC heuristic with per-patch 2-DOF affine photometric (delete the transient detector; divide pyramids by exposure on TUM-VI); add a frame-level RANSAC track-quality gate with the IMU rotation prior (the load-bearing fix for aperture-slide-under-blur, not the residual-norm gate); and raise the feature budget (num_points_cell/grid, detection-only CLAHE copy, 3 px tile overlap, re-enable cornerSubPix).
  8. 8
    LAST on the reloc side, fix VPR input geometry: undistort the fisheye to virtual pinhole view(s) before embedding (rank 8) and calibrate retrieval thresholds per-model with a top1/top2 ratio or per-query z-score. Deliberately last because until the downstream geometric gates (steps 2 and 4) are open, retrieval-quality improvements cannot show measurable benefit -- the gates mask them, which is precisely why MegaLoc showed no gain over DBoW2.
